Honnemaradu - Golden Lake

Honnemaradu is a tourist place situated on the backwaters of the River Sharavathi in Sagara taluk, Karnataka, India. The name "Honnemardu" is derived from the Honne tree, but it translates to "Golden Lake," possibly referring to its location on the backwaters of the Sharavathi river. It is in Sagara taluk, about 35 km from Sagara on the way to Jog Falls, 12 km from Talaguppa, and 392 km from Bangalore.

Honnemaradu is a small village located on the hills that overlook the Linganamakki Reservoir. The reservoir is also known as Honnemaradu after the village. In the middle of this water body, there is a small island that attracts campers. In addition to adventure activities, Honnemaradu is popular with birdwatchers who can spend hours watching and identifying the variety of birds and butterflies. Honnemaradu camp is managed by a team of nature enthusiasts called 'Adventurers'.

Honnemaradu is tucked away in the lush greenery of the Western Ghats along the Sharavati backwaters near Sagara. Encompassing an area of 50 km by 80 km, the reservoir at Honnemaradu, or "Golden Lake," is known for its views of the sunrise and sunset.

Water Sports and Activities

Trekking is only permitted along roads in a designated area. Some of the water sports activities here include boating and kayaking, but it is only permitted with a guide. Swimming is not allowed, and there is no longer equipment for wind rafting.

Nearby Waterfalls

Jog Falls is created by the Sharavathi River, plummeting down from a great height of 289 ft, making it the highest waterfall in India. Dabbe Falls, Sagara, is located 30 km from Jog Falls, nestled in the mountains near Hosagadde.
