About Apsarakonda

Apsarakonda, which signifies the Pond of Angels, is a thriving tourist town located 476 kilometres from Bengaluru.
The myth behind the waterfall’s name is that it was the chosen place for the angels to bathe and rest.
Hidden behind the Narasimha temple, Apsarakonda is blessed with gorgeous blue waters, lush green fields, and the chirping of birds.
The pleasant beauty of this place will leave you spellbound.

Attractions

Apsarakonda is an emerging tourist village near Honnavar, in the district of Uttara Kannada, Karnataka, India.
It is situated 8 km from the Honnavar bus stand.

Etymology: Apsarakonda means Pond of Angels.
This is named after a pond facing the beach. The legend associated with the name of the waterfall is that it was the chosen place of the angels to take a bath and relax.

Key Attractions:

  • Maha Ganapati and Ugra Narasimha temple
  • Pandava cave of historical significance
  • Park near the falls maintained by the forest department
  • Kelginoor lagoon near the beach

Apsarakonda is known for its unexplored beaches.

Activities

Immerse yourself in the natural beauty and cultural richness of Apsarakonda with various activities, including temple visits, cave exploration, and enjoying the serene beaches.
