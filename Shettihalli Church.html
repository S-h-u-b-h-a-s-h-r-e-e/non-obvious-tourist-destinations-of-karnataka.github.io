Shettihalli Church: A Submerged Architectural Marvel

History and Submersion

Built in 1860 for wealthy British estate owners, the church faced an unforeseen fate in 1960. The construction of the Hemavati Dam and Reservoir led to the abandonment of the church, submerging it underwater during the monsoon season. Despite its submerged state, the Shettihalli Church has become a popular tourist spot, attracting visitors who come to witness the spectacle, often referred to as The Submerged Church or The Floating Church.

Visiting the Church

To fully experience the surreal beauty of Shettihalli Church, visitors are encouraged to make two trips. The first, between July and October, allows one to witness the church partially submerged in water. The second, between December and May, reveals the church grounds as the water level recedes.

Travel Information

  • Distance: Shettihalli to Bangalore is approximately 200 km by road.
  • Transportation: Buses are available for easy access to Shettihalli Church. Trains run daily from Yashwantpur railway station to Hassan, Karnataka, the nearest town located about 40 km from Shettihalli.

Digital Preservation

The church has been digitized, and a 3D model is available to the public domain, providing an immersive virtual reality experience. This digitization effort was undertaken by axesmap.
